Many of my earliest memories are from trying to solve problems on the command-line. Now I'm a professional programmer and it doesn't seem so different after all. Those early years had a playful quality to them. Perhaps the past always looks like that? Still, there's something precious in it. Something worth sharing.

@QuiteClose

I'm a (mostly) self-taught programmer. Here are some of the resources that made the biggest impact on me:

MIT 6.001: Structure & Interpretation of Computer Programs
A lecture series (and book) that finally challenged some old habits.
The Art of Unix Programming
Learn about the environment.
Don't Make Me Think
Fundamentals of UI useability.
Why's (Poignant) Guide to Ruby
An invitation to program for fun.
The C Programming Language
All too familiar with the first N pages of this book.
TDD in Python: Obey the Testing Goat!
Testing made my code better.
